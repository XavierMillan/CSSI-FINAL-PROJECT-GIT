What is Student-Oriented Coding (SoC)?

Student-Oriented Coding is an educational website whose purpose is to inspire and educate K-5 students to learn about Computer Science principles. With easy-to-play games and mini-lessons, it makes learning CS fun and accessible for students.

Learn more about us

CS education still remains inaccessible to many students

  • Only 26% of national elementary schools offer computer programming classes.1
  • Almost 75% of California schools with the highest percentage of underrepresented students of color don't offer any CS classes.2
  • Research shows that learning computer science at a young age improves computational thinking and problem-solving skills.
  • Around 30% of K-8 schools offer foundational computer science in across 19 states.3
  • Underrepresented groups, including English language learners, students with disabilities, and economically disadvantaged students, are less likely to have access to CS curriculum.

How SoC can help

Made for students. By students.

Our mini-lessons and fun games encourages students to learn important computer science principles such as variables and basic JavaScript. Use them in your classrooms to help bridge the gap in CS education.
